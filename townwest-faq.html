The money
How exactly do I get paid?

Three lines. Finder: a signing bonus per owner you bring, plus a recurring cut of our management fee for as long as that home stays on the platform. Boots: a percent of gross on homes your crew runs ground for. Maintenance: you keep your vendor markup on repairs.

You can do one, two, or all three. The calculator on the offer page lets you model any mix.

Are the rates fixed?

No. The numbers shown are illustrative, built on our real Tucson averages. We paper exact splits once we pick the first units. The bands: finder recurring ~10-15% of our fee, boots ~7-10% of gross. We'll land where it's fair for the work you do.

Is the finder cut really forever?

For as long as the home stays on the platform, yes. You bring an owner once, you earn on that home every month it's managed. That's why bringing more compounds.

The work
What do I actually have to do?

Depends on the path. Finder Light: just refer owners. We run everything. Full Operator: refer owners + your crew handles turnover quality, owner face-time, and access, and routes maintenance through your vendors. We handle pricing, guest comms, channels, reviews, reporting.

What does Welgo do that I don't?

The heavy, scalable ops: dynamic pricing, 24/7 guest support, listing across Airbnb + Booking + VRBO + Expedia + direct, review management, owner reporting, safety escalation. That's the engine that takes a home from ~15% occupancy to ~61%.

Can I use my existing crew?

Yes — that's the point. Your housekeepers and vendors stay yours. You're the local operator; we're the engine behind you. No need to hire or rebuild.

My brand + owners
Do my owners become Welgo's?

Not unless you want that. Default is white-label: Town West stays the brand they see, the relationship stays yours, Welgo is the invisible engine. If you'd rather show the partnership, we co-brand "Town West, powered by Welgo." Your call.

What happens to my existing contracts?

Start by renting the engine — keep your contracts as they are. If we both want to go deeper later, there's an operator-owner path with equity. No requirement up front. We start simple.

The risk
What if it doesn't work?

You risk nothing to find out. Pick 2-3 of your units. We run them for 90 days. You see real numbers. On those first units we guarantee a revenue floor — if a home comes in under target, we cover the gap. Scale only if it works. $0 to find out.

Why is Welgo stepping back from Arizona?

We're moving from running every home ourselves to powering local operators who run the ground. Same engine, more homes, less of our direct labor. You're the kind of local partner that model is built for.

Getting started
How do we start?

Bring me 2-3 units. We connect them, run them 90 days, and you watch the numbers. If they prove out, we scale and paper the full operator agreement.

How many operators are you taking?

Five Tucson operators total. You'd be among the first — early operators lock the best terms and territory.
