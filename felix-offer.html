How you get paid, in plain terms

You run the local ground and source owners. We run the engine. Three lines, each tied to real work.

1. Boots: you run the ground

What you get: 6% of each home's gross, plus up to 2% more in green weeks when you hit the service bar (fast acks, full photo proof, low complaints). Your crew does turnovers, quality, owner face, access.

Why pay for quality: owner reviews drive occupancy, which drives everyone's income. The green-week bonus rewards the homes you run clean. On a $29,314 home: 6% is ~$1,759, 8% green is ~$2,345.

2. Finder: owners you bring

What you get: a recurring slice of the Welgo fee on owners you source, and it steps up with volume: 10% on your first 10 homes, 12% on 11 to 25, 15% on 26+. Plus a signing bonus per owner, paid on the home's first booking revenue.

Why stepped: the more you bring, the higher your rate. It rewards scaling, not dabbling.

3. Maintenance: your repair margin

What you get: repairs route through your vendors, you keep your markup, capped at 15% to protect the owner. That cap is a selling point: their manager cannot gouge them on repairs.

The math: annual maintenance spend × your markup. Adjust both below.
