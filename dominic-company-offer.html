Buyout or buy-in path

If the operating fit is obvious, we can talk about what buying into or buying Welgo Arizona could look like. That is path 3, not the starting point.

This is not a final legal offer. The earlier $85K idea needs a separate first-principles rebuild before it is used. We would need to define what transfers, what is excluded, how Hostaway, contracts, furniture, brand, liabilities, IP, tax, legal review, and owner relationships are handled.

Ownership value

If Dominic earns into equity, the important question is not only yearly operator cash. It is what the Arizona managed-listing company becomes as listings grow.

1
Listing economics
LTV and CAC matter
One listing is not just the PM fee. The model also includes direct-booking revenue, resolution revenue, upsells, scale margin, retention, and acquisition cost.
2
Company value
Value grows with managed listings and revenue quality
At 100 homes, the current model shows about $2.1M to $3.7M of Welgo AZ company value. That uses 50% direct mix, 5% resolution revenue, scale margin, and a 3.0x to 5.5x cash-flow multiple.
3
Dominic's piece
Ownership turns operating work into asset value
At 10% ownership, that 100-home case would imply about $205K to $366K of equity value, before any separate buyout logic.
4
Pressure test
The high end has to be earned
If contracts are weak, owners can churn, the team depends on one person, or direct bookings are not proven, the value should compress. Gross revenue alone is not company value.
