The sequence

Do the small thing first. Then decide the bigger structure.

Real deals reduce risk in order. Relationship, proof, terms, diligence, then integration.

  1. Walk one Tucson unit with Shannon.
    Look at it like you are responsible for the cleaner, the owner, the review, and the next guest.
  2. Run a narrow pilot.
    Cleaning, inspection, photo proof, small fixes, supplies, and issue closure on a small lane.
  3. Review the scorecard.
    Speed, standards, cleaner accountability, owner confidence, and how much remote ops can trust the handoff.
  4. Pick the path.
    Operator/CEO path, listings under Welgo, or a company-level buy-in or buyout conversation.
  5. Scale only after proof.
    Route density, underperforming listings, owner referrals, and small-operator rollups come after the ground works.
