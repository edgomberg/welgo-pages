The money
How do I make money on this versus just renting it out?

Short-term done well usually beats a static rent on the right unit, because nightly pricing flexes with demand and you capture peak weekends, events, and seasons. You keep ownership and the upside. We take a percentage only when the unit earns.

What does it actually cost me?

The draft P&L model uses an 18.75% management fee. For the 2025 packet, the fee base is accommodation + cleaning + pet income, net of platform/host fees. Resolution revenue and guest upsells are excluded. Final contract language should match this before signature.

How do platform fees affect payout and the management fee?

In this draft model, platform/host fees reduce the management-fee calculation base and reduce owner payout. If the signed agreement uses a gross fee base instead, the management fee and owner payout will change. That fee-base choice needs to be locked in Exhibit B.

What about direct bookings?

Direct bookings are not part of launch scope. They may be added later by written addendum after Welgo proves guest screening, rental agreement, payment, tax, insurance, damage, and calendar controls on a lower-risk pilot.

What is the performance bonus?

On top of the fees, we earn a bonus only when your units out-earn comparable Tucson homes, never just because the market rose. We measure your revenue per available night against a fixed set of 8 to 10 comparable listings we lock with you. Beat that comp set and we share 25% of the revenue above it. Do not beat it and the bonus is zero. Upside-only for us, so we earn more only when you earn more.

Walk me through a real month.

The monthly statement should show revenue, platform fees, the management-fee calculation base, Welgo's management fee, cleaning collected, cleaning remitted to the local operator, selected owner costs, and estimated owner distribution. You receive a clean statement by the 10th showing every line, and payout by the 15th after reconciliation.

Illustrative only. Real numbers depend on unit, season, source close, and final contract terms.

Who keeps the guest upsells?

We do. Upsells are extra services we build and sell to guests on top of the stay, like early check-in or a pet fee. That is new money that does not exist today and does not come out of your nightly revenue. Your fee is calculated on nightly booking revenue only, never on upsells.

When and how do I get paid?

Monthly. Report by the 10th for the prior month, payout by the 15th, after reconciliation. Every dollar shows on the statement.

The risk
Short-term income is lumpy. Why take that over a steady check?

Because the upside can be higher than a static rent when pricing, calendar, reviews, and operations are managed well. The 2025 model shows the base case slightly above the old fixed rent, and the upside case materially above it. That upside is not guaranteed. It depends on final unit scope, source reconciliation, pricing control, calendar control, and execution.

What if Welgo underperforms?

Welgo is not offering an uncapped revenue guarantee. Welgo can agree to a performance framework for work it controls, such as pricing, calendar strategy, listing setup, channel management, guest communication, and reporting. Any remedy should be a capped fee credit, not an open-ended revenue make-whole.

What is the benchmark you measure against?

The benchmark should be locked in writing before any guarantee, bonus, or performance framework turns on. The comparison set should match the final signed unit scope, not a generic market average. Owner-blocked nights, pricing control, channel control, and unit readiness all need to be accounted for.

Am I on the hook if a guest gets hurt or trashes the place?

You carry STR-appropriate insurance and we name ourselves as additional insured where the policy allows. Damage claims and guest disputes are handled by us. Liability for the building and ownership stays with ownership, the same as any property you own, except where we are grossly negligent.

The work
How much of my time does this take?

Almost none. You give us access once. After that you read a monthly report and collect a payout. Guests, cleaning, pricing, reviews, maintenance calls, all on us, 24/7.

Do I have to deal with guests or middle-of-the-night problems?

No. That is the whole point. We run guest communication around the clock and dispatch maintenance. You never get the 2am call.

What do you need from me to start?

Access: listing or platform access, lock access, insurance proof, permit and tax details, photos, and your payout account. The full checklist is in the agreement. Once it is complete, we launch within 72 hours.

The control
Who owns the listings and reviews if we ever split?

You own your properties and your property-specific listing assets. If we part ways, we hand over a full package: calendars, reservations, guest info, codes, vendors, statements. Some platforms limit transferring review history, and we cooperate fully, but no manager can guarantee a platform will move reviews.

Can I block dates for myself or family?

Yes. Owner-blocked nights are yours. We just account for them in the occupancy math so a night you took off the market does not count against us.

Can I get out if I do not like it?

Either side can terminate under the final agreement terms. If owner cancels early for reasons other than Welgo underperformance, owner reimburses documented launch costs up to the agreed cap. The cap should be locked in the final agreement before signature.

What can you spend without asking me?

Up to $500 per incident on routine supplies and small fixes, so we are not chasing you for every light bulb. Anything above $500 we clear with you first, except a true emergency like a leak or a lockout where waiting causes damage.

Your home, protected
How is my property protected?

$1M damage and $3M liability protection on every stay, layered on top of your own STR insurance. Every guest is screened: ID match, fraud score, and party-risk check on every booking. And you get local eyes: an independent inspector audits a sample of turnovers, plus a quarterly 40-point walk-through that catches wear and damage early, before it becomes a bill.

Plans and what is included
Is full-service the only option, or are there lighter plans?

Three plans. This 8-unit discussion is modeled as full-service unless the final agreement says otherwise. The general menu:

Full-Service, 18.5% standard (your custom 8th St terms are set in your agreement) . we run everything, you collect a check. Industry standard runs about 25%.
Co-Host, 12% . we run revenue and guests, you keep cleaning and maintenance. Industry standard is 15%.
Software-led, 6% . our pricing and listing engine, you run the ground game.

One transparent fee per plan, every line on your dashboard, no nickel-and-diming.

What is included in full-service versus co-host?

Both plans include: multi-platform listings, listing creation and rank optimization, dynamic pricing, guest vetting (ID and fraud), 24/7 guest communications, and an owner dashboard with occupancy, ADR, and RevPAR.

Full-service adds: cleaning and housekeeping, linens and hotel toiletries, professional photography at cost, independent inspections, quarterly preventative maintenance, quick-fix repairs under $500, and permit and lodging-tax filing and remittance. On co-host, you book your own cleaner and tech and we send you the permit and tax links.
