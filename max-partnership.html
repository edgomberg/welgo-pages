Max Martin × Welgo · The bigger idea

Beyond managing your units: a partnership where we both own more.

Managing your rentals is the first deal. This is the second, and it is optional. A way for us to build real value together, where you end up owning a piece of a growing company and I put my own money and work into your buildings.

Draft for discussion. A starting-point proposal, not a signed offer. Every number is illustrative until we set a real property valuation with an appraiser and a real-estate attorney plus CPA paper the structure. Nothing here is legal or tax advice.

The idea in one line

I put capital and operations into your buildings and earn a slice of them over time. You take a slice of Welgo, so you grow with the company, not just your own units.

Two things move, in opposite directions. My money and work flow into your real estate. A piece of my company flows to you. Both of us end up with more skin in the game and more upside.

What each of us brings

I bring

  • Up to $100,000 of capital into your units over about 24 months. Furniture, renovations, upgrades that lift both nightly rate and the value of the building. Phased as we go, not a lump sum.
  • The full short-term-rental engine, run for years, lifting what the units earn.
  • The work. You do not operate anything.

You bring

  • The buildings, into one partnership entity, at an agreed value.
  • You keep title control: major decisions, refinance, and sale stay your call.
  • That is it. You contribute the asset, I contribute the capital and the engine.

What each of us earns

  • I earn a vesting slice of the property, in the range of 10 to 20% of the combined Santa Rita + Hoff entity. It is earned, not given: it vests as I actually deploy capital and hit performance milestones over 24 months. Every dollar I put in is tracked and counts toward my share, set against the agreed property value.
  • You earn a vesting slice of Welgo, around 5% of the management company, as our anchor owner-partner. As Welgo grows past your units (more owners, more homes), you ride that growth, not just your own rentals. Room to grow your stake if you bring in more.

Both slices vest over time and are valued separately: mine off your property's value, yours off Welgo's value. They are not a swap or a barter, they are two earned stakes.

Why this is safe for you

  • You cannot lose the building. My equity vests as I perform and fund. If I underperform or walk away, the unvested portion is forfeited and reverts to you. You keep title and control the whole time.
  • You stay in charge. Major decisions, new debt, and any sale need your sign-off. This is a partnership, not a handover.
  • Your capital risk is low. The capex is mine, not a loan against you. Your downside is protected; your upside is the lift in cash flow and value plus a stake in a growing firm.

How it is structured (kept simple)

  • One partnership LLC holds the Santa Rita and Hoff units. You contribute the buildings at an agreed, appraiser-set value.
  • My capital is tracked in a capital account; it converts to my vesting % at a formula we lock up front, so there is never a valuation argument later.
  • Welgo runs the rentals under the management agreement (the first deal); your guaranteed income sits there.
  • You receive a small, vesting equity grant in Welgo as the anchor owner-partner.
  • We both stay active, you with veto and full books access, me running operations. A real-estate attorney and CPA paper all of it before any money or title moves.

Why now

I am already moving furniture and capital out of units we are exiting elsewhere. That capital can flow straight into your buildings instead of sitting idle. The sooner we start, the sooner both the cash flow and the value start compounding for both of us.

Next step

No rush and no pressure. Read it, sit with it. If the shape interests you, we get an appraiser on the buildings and an attorney to paper a clean version. The management deal stands on its own either way.
