A steady check every month. All of it your money.

No pitch, just how it works. We run your units, you get a level monthly check your bank can count on, and you keep every dollar the units earn. Here is the plain version, plus a couple of ways you can choose to be paid.

The idea in plain words

  • We pay you a set amount every month, same date. That is the number your bank sees.
  • We hold a little of your money back into your own reserve. Think of it as a savings cushion that belongs to you, not us.
  • A slow month, the reserve tops up your check so it does not drop. A strong month, the reserve fills back up.
  • It is all your money. We never keep the difference, and we never pay you more than your units actually earn. No gimmick. You see every dollar on a monthly statement.

Pick how you want to be paid

All three are your money. The only difference is how much we hold back as your cushion.

Most owners pick this

1. Steady 80% + reserve

We pay you a level check at about 80% of your average net. The other 20% builds your reserve, which keeps your check level through slow months. Steadiest for your bank, and you still get the held-back money (it is yours, in reserve, and trued up).

2. Steadier 90% + thinner reserve

A bigger check at about 90%, smaller cushion. More cash in hand now, but the reserve runs thin, so in a long slow stretch the check has to step down sooner.

3. Take it all, variable

You take your full net every month, no reserve, no smoothing. Simplest, but the number moves up and down, which your bank may not like.

The honest part

We do not promise a number bigger than your units earn. If a long stretch genuinely underperforms and your reserve runs out, the set check steps down to match reality, and we show you exactly why, line by line. What we guarantee is the operations and the consistency: a level check, a real cushion built from your own strong months, and a clear statement every time. You keep all your profit; you carry the real performance of your own units, the same as you do today.

What we sign (two separate documents)

  • 1. The Management Agreement. Covers how we run the units: the 18.75% fee, the operating costs, the services, the term (12 months, then month to month, 60 days notice). Standard management terms. Not a lease, no new personal guarantee.
  • 2. The Payment & Reserve Agreement (separate). Covers only how you get paid: your chosen option (the set check), the reserve held from your own money, the monthly statement, and how the number resets if a long stretch underperforms. This is the piece your lender cares about, and it stands as its own document so it is clean to show the bank.

Plus a one-page letter your lender can file showing your set monthly amount.
