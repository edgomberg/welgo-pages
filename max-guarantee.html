The shape, in three lines

  • You get a fixed payment every month, same date. That is the number your bank sees. It does not move with bookings.
  • Underneath, it is management, not a lease. We collect, run the units, take a fee. You keep ownership, no new personal guarantee.
  • We carry the swing. Busy months and slow months are our problem to manage, not yours.

How the money moves each month

  1. We collect. Guest payments land in Welgo's account.
  2. We pay the unit's real costs + our fee (cleaning, utilities, supplies, maintenance, and the 18.75% management fee).
  3. We pay you the fixed amount, on a set date, whatever the month actually did.
  4. We reconcile every quarter (next section).

Who covers the gap when a month is slow?

This is the part that matters. There are two different situations, and they are covered by two different people.

1. A timing dip, covered by your own good months

Most slow months are just timing. A strong month earned more than your fixed payment, so we held the extra. When a slow month comes, we pay your fixed number using that held surplus. This is your own money, paid evenly. Welgo is not covering anything here, just smoothing the timing so your payment is flat.

2. A real shortfall, covered by Welgo

If a whole quarter actually earns less than your floor (not just one slow month, the real total falls short), there is no surplus of yours to draw on. Welgo pays the difference out of its own pocket. That is the actual guarantee. It is capped, and Welgo only earns it back from future quarters that beat the floor. It is never billed to you. Your payment never moves either way.

Setting the real floor

The $10,000 above is an example. We set the real floor off your actual numbers, not a guess: we run one real month on one unit in the statement format above, confirm the true net across all eight units, then set a floor we can hold every month. If the numbers support it, the floor matches your current $10,000 so your bank sees no change.

The honest part: your true net depends on real operating costs, especially utilities and cleaning frequency. We confirm it on a real statement before any floor is locked.
