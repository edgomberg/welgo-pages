You always get 100% of your true net

This is the part to be clear on. The options below only change the timing of your money, never the total. Welgo never keeps your surplus, and never pays you more than the units earn. Over each quarter, the total paid to you equals your true net. The reserve is just a buffer of your own money that keeps the monthly check level.

Two ways to take it. You pick.

Same money either way (100% of your net over time). The only choice is whether you want it steady, or tracking the units more closely.

Recommended for your bank

1. A fixed monthly check

A set number, same date every month, that does not move with bookings. We keep a small buffer from your strong months so slow months never change your check, and each quarter we pay you everything above it. Predictable, bankable, and you still get every dollar your units earn.

2. A number that tracks the units

We recalculate your payment each quarter to follow what the units actually earn. It can run higher than the fixed check in strong stretches, but it moves with the season, so it is less predictable for a lender. Same total over time, just less smooth.

How we keep the fixed check steady (the buffer, the timing) is our job to run, not yours to manage. You just pick steady or tracking.

You are never paid more than your units earn, and never less over time

The fixed check is set a touch below your average net, so the small buffer we hold from strong months is always enough to keep it level through slow ones. In the rare case a long stretch genuinely underperforms, the check recalculates down to match reality, shown line by line. Welgo never tops up the difference from its own pocket and never keeps your surplus. It is always your own money, just timed to land evenly.
